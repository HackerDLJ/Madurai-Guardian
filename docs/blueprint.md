# **App Name**: Madurai Guardian

## Core Features:

- Incident Reporting: Users can quickly submit reports of urban cleanliness issues (e.g., trash, overflowing bins, illegal dumping), including location, description, and photo attachments.
- AI-Powered Report Augmentation: An AI tool assists users by suggesting precise report categories or enriching descriptions based on uploaded images, improving the quality and consistency of incident data.
- Interactive City Map: Visualizes reported incidents on a real-time interactive map, allowing all users (residents, municipality, visitors) to see hotspots and areas requiring attention across Madurai.
- Report Status Tracking: Users can view the real-time status of their submitted reports (e.g., pending, acknowledged, in progress, resolved) and receive updates.
- Community Awareness Hub: Provides access to educational content, local waste management guidelines, civic responsibility tips, and municipal announcements related to urban cleanliness.
- Location-Based Notifications: Alerts users about new cleanliness reports in their immediate vicinity or sends targeted updates on reports they've submitted or are following.

## Style Guidelines:

- The app uses a light color scheme to convey cleanliness and clarity. Primary elements will use a fresh, moderate blue (#3399CC) inspired by themes of communication and environmental care, avoiding harshness to evoke calm trustworthiness. The background color is a heavily desaturated blue-grey (#F2F6F8), creating a subtle and clean canvas for content. An accent color of rich cyan (#129E9E) provides visual contrast and draws attention to interactive elements, suggesting innovation and responsiveness.
- For a contemporary and universally legible experience in line with Google's design principles, 'Inter' (sans-serif) will be used for all text, ensuring excellent readability across various screen sizes and information densities for both headlines and body text.
- Clean, outlined, and recognizable vector-based icons will be utilized, aligning with modern Google Material Design language for clear and intuitive navigation. Icons should be easily understandable and communicate their purpose effectively to a diverse user base.
- A mobile-first approach will dictate clear, structured, and consistent layouts that prioritize content legibility and ease of interaction on smaller screens. Elements like floating action buttons, cards for grouping information, and ample white space will adhere to Google's intuitive and efficient UI/UX principles for an 'Original 2026' feel.
- Subtle, deliberate animations and transitions will be implemented for feedback on user interactions (e.g., ripple effects, loading indicators) and to enhance the sense of a dynamic, responsive ecosystem. These will follow Material Design guidelines for natural motion and visual continuity without being distracting.
